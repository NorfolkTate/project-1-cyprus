Visit Cyprus

The jewel of the Mediterranean

Welcome to the enchanting island of Cyprus, where ancient history meets vibrant modernity, and natural beauty beckons at every turn. Nestled in the heart of the Mediterranean, Cyprus is a captivating destination that promises a diverse and unforgettable experience. Renowned for its sun-kissed beaches, crystalline waters, and a climate that invites year-round exploration, Cyprus is a haven for sunseekers and nature enthusiasts alike. But this island is more than just a beach paradise; it boasts a rich tapestry of history and culture, with archaeological wonders dating back millennia.

History of the conflict

The Cyprus problem centers around the protracted political dispute on the island of Cyprus in the Eastern Mediterranean. Stemming from its colonial history, Cyprus gained independence from British rule in 1960, establishing the Republic of Cyprus with power-sharing arrangements between the Greek Cypriot majority and Turkish Cypriot minority. However, intercommunal tensions led to violence in the 1960s, prompting a Turkish military intervention in 1974. This resulted in the de facto division of the island, with the northern part controlled by Turkish Cypriots and the southern part by Greek Cypriots, separated by the Green Line, monitored by UN peacekeepers. While the Republic of Cyprus is internationally recognized, the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus (TRNC), declared in 1983, is only recognized by Turkey. Numerous international efforts, including the Annan Plan in 2004, have aimed at finding a comprehensive resolution, but a lasting solution has remained elusive. The division continues to be a source of tension, with distinct political structures for each community, the presence of Turkish troops in the north, and disputes over property rights. The United Nations and the international community, including the European Union, have been involved in facilitating negotiations to reunify the island, but as of my last knowledge update in January 2022, a comprehensive resolution had not been reached. It's advisable to seek the latest information for any developments since then.
